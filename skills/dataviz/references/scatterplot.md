# Scatterplot

**Use when**: exploring or demonstrating correlation between two numeric variables; identifying clusters, outliers, or distributional patterns.  
**Avoid when**: the audience is unfamiliar with statistical charts and no live narration is possible; the relationship is unclear or absent; a simpler comparison (ranking, trend) would communicate the point.

## Key rules

- Zero baseline not required — axes should frame the data range, not start at zero
- Add quadrant lines, reference lines, or annotations to guide interpretation; raw scatterplots are exploratory, annotated ones are explanatory
- Use transparency or jitter to handle overplotting (many overlapping points)
- Label only key data points, not all — names on every point create noise
- Always note that correlation ≠ causation in the title or annotation when the relationship could be misread

## Anti-patterns

- Showing a scatterplot to a general audience without any annotation or narration — they don't know what pattern to look for
- Forcing a trend line when the relationship isn't linear or when no meaningful trend exists
- Connected scatterplot (time encoded as movement) without explicitly labeling the time path — direction of movement is invisible otherwise
